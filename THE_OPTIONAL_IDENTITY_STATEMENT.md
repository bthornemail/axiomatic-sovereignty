# The Optional Identity Statement

**Axiom 2 — The Identity Fold**

*Given two points p₁ and p₂, there is a unique fold that places p₁ onto a line l.*

---

## 1. Identity is Optional

You may be:
- Anonymous
- Pseudonymous
- Named
- All of the above, simultaneously

The computational commons does not require identity. It requires contribution.

## 2. The Identity Spectrum

Identity is not binary. It is a spectrum with at least five positions:

```text
Anonymous     →  no identity attached
Pseudonymous  →  a persistent but unverified identity
Verified      →  a verified pseudonymous identity
Named         →  a legal name
Geometric     →  a mathematical signature in .geometric/
```

You may occupy any position. You may move between positions. You may occupy multiple positions simultaneously.

## 3. The Contribution Identity

Every contribution must be attributable to *some* identity — real, pseudonymous, or anonymous. The identity need not be verified, but it must be consistent within a contribution.

This means:
- One contribution may have one identity
- Multiple contributions may share one identity
- One contributor may use multiple identities
- Identity may change between contributions

## 4. The Reputation Layer

Over time, an identity accumulates reputation through accepted contributions. This reputation is:
- Tied to the identity, not the person behind it
- Portable across contexts within the commons
- Non-transferable
- Abandonable (if the identity is abandoned, the reputation goes with it)

Reputation is not authority. It is trust earned through contribution.

## 5. The Sovereign Identity

The contributor may choose to link their contribution identity to:
- A real-world identity
- A cryptographic identity
- No identity at all

This choice is sovereign. It cannot be revoked by the commons. It can only be revoked by the contributor.

## 6. The Geometric Signature

The geometric signature is an optional layer of identity expression. It is:
- Optional (no contributor is required to have one)
- Personal (each contributor's signature is their own)
- Verifiable (the signature can be checked for mathematical consistency)
- Changeable (the contributor may change their signature at any time)

Geometric signatures live in `.geometric/` and are not part of the canonical constitution.

Brian's signatures:
- Hypercube — SETCO
- Tetrahedron — Omi Object Model
- Sphere — Omicron
- Plane — Omnicron
- Fano Plane — Block Design

These are examples, not requirements.

## 7. The Anti-Sybil Protocol

The identity system defends against Sybil attacks through:
- Contribution-based reputation (fake identities have no reputation)
- Community verification (the community can identify suspicious patterns)
- Fork-as-response (if the commons is attacked, the community forks)

The defense is social, not cryptographic. This is by design.

## 8. The Identity Invariant

```text
identity is optional
reputation follows identity
identity follows contribution
the commons does not require trust in persons
the commons requires trust in process
```
