## Freedom, Autonomy, Sovereignty, and Reciprocity

Axiomatic Sovereignty is grounded in freedom, autonomy, and sovereignty, exercised in appreciation of reciprocity.

Freedom is the capacity to receive, consider, reject, preserve, modify, or propagate knowledge without imposed domination.

Autonomy is the capacity of a conscious being or community to reason, choose, and act without being absorbed into an authority they did not freely accept.

Sovereignty is the responsibility to govern one’s own participation, conduct, dependencies, and consequences.

Reciprocity is the recognition that sovereign capacity is often made possible by what others have preserved, discovered, verified, taught, corrected, or propagated.

Reciprocity does not create a debt of obedience.

It does not require submission to the originator.

It does not convert gratitude into ownership.

It is an appreciation of relation.

```text
Freedom permits reception.

Autonomy permits independent judgment.

Sovereignty carries responsibility.

Reciprocity honors the relation that made continuation possible.
```

A person may reciprocate by preserving an unchanged foundation.

Another may reciprocate by correcting an error.

Another may translate it.

Another may teach it.

Another may create a fork.

Another may contribute a proof, warning, implementation, or account of consequences.

Another may simply pass it forward faithfully.

No single form of reciprocity is universally required.

The meaningful principle is that what is received should not be enclosed, falsely claimed, or used to subordinate those from whom it came or those to whom it may pass.

Propagation is the principal authority because it continues sovereign capacity.

Backpropagation is the moral authority because it returns consequence, correction, and witness.

Non-interference preserves inherited authority.

Reciprocity appreciates the relation among all three.

```text
Propagation carries capacity forward.

Backpropagation returns consequence.

Non-interference preserves what was received.

Reciprocity acknowledges and strengthens the relation.
```

The individual is not dissolved into the collective.

The collective is not granted dominion over the individual.

The common foundation arises through freely maintained relations among sovereign conscious beings.

Its measure is not obedience.

Its measure is whether more beings become capable of understanding, choosing, building, correcting, and helping others do the same.

The canonical principle is:

```text
Freedom without domination.

Autonomy without isolation.

Sovereignty without supremacy.

Reciprocity without coercion.
```
