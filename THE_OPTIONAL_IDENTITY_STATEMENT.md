# The Optional Identity Statement

**Axiom 2 - The Identity Fold**

*Given two points p1 and p2, there is a unique fold that places p1 onto a line l.*

---

## 1. Machine Anchor

The front matter above is the machine-readable anchor for this branch. It gives downstream tools a small, explicit set of stable references without making this repository depend on a specific runtime, framework, or institution.

Forks may change these fields to describe their own namespace, composition mode, and layer layout. A changed anchor creates responsibility for the changed meaning; it does not create ownership over the original foundation.

## 2. Identity Is Optional

You may be:

- Anonymous
- Pseudonymous
- Named
- All of the above, simultaneously

The common foundation does not require identity. It requires contribution.

## 3. Authorship Is Not Ownership

A contribution should be attributable to some identity, even if that identity is anonymous or pseudonymous. Attribution preserves provenance and accountability.

Attribution does not make the contributor an owner of the foundation. Authorship records who introduced a claim, translation, proof, correction, or fork. Continuity follows propagation, consequence, verification, and backpropagation.

## 4. The Identity Spectrum

Identity is not binary. It is a spectrum:

```text
Anonymous     -> no persistent public identity attached
Pseudonymous  -> a persistent but unverified identity
Verified      -> a verified pseudonymous identity
Named         -> a public name
Geometric     -> an optional mathematical signature
```

You may occupy any position. You may move between positions. You may occupy multiple positions simultaneously.

## 5. The Contribution Identity

Every contribution should be attributable to the identity used for that contribution. The identity need not be verified, but it should be consistent within the contribution record.

This means:

- One contribution may have one identity
- Multiple contributions may share one identity
- One contributor may use multiple identities
- Identity may change between contributions

## 6. The Reputation Layer

Over time, an identity may accumulate reputation through accepted contributions. This reputation is:

- Tied to the identity, not the person behind it
- Portable across contexts within the common foundation
- Non-transferable
- Abandonable

Reputation is not control. It is trust earned through contribution.

## 7. The Sovereign Identity

The contributor may choose to link their contribution identity to:

- A real-world identity
- A cryptographic identity
- No persistent identity at all

This choice is sovereign. It cannot be revoked by the common foundation. It can only be revoked by the contributor.

## 8. The Identity Invariant

```text
identity is optional
authorship preserves provenance
authorship does not create ownership over the foundation
reputation follows identity
identity follows contribution
the common foundation does not require trust in persons
the common foundation requires trust in process
```
