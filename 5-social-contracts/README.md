# Layer 5: Social Contracts

**Purpose:** Group intent — what the community agrees to uphold.

**Format:** Any programmable template — smart contracts, policy-as-code, or plain Markdown with clear conditions.

---

## What This Layer Defines

Social Contracts specify the agreements that the community makes with itself. These are not laws. They are promises — enforceable only by reputation and trust.

```text

social contracts may be amended by community consent
social contracts may be forked by individual dissent
social contracts expire when the community dissolves
```

# Commons Charter

**Contract:** The charter of the computational commons.

## Purpose

This charter establishes the social contract among contributors to the computational commons. It is not enforceable by law. It is enforceable by reputation and trust.

## Articles

### Article 1: Contribution

Every contributor may contribute to the commons, provided their contribution passes all five gates.

### Article 2: Sovereignty

Every contributor is sovereign. No contributor may be compelled to contribute, to fork, or to merge.

### Article 3: Forking

Every contributor may fork the commons at any time, for any reason. Forking is sovereignty, not fragmentation.

### Article 4: Attribution

Every contribution must be attributed to an identity (real, pseudonymous, or anonymous). Attribution is optional but not secret.

### Article 5: Maintenance

Every contributor is encouraged to maintain their contributions. If a contributor can no longer maintain a contribution, they should transfer it to another contributor or mark it as dormant.

### Article 6: Amendment

This charter may be amended by community consent. Amendments must be recorded in the branch registry.

### Article 7: Dissolution

This charter expires when the last contributor leaves the commons. At that point, the commons becomes a historical artifact.
