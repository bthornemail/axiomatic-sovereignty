# Foundation Charter

**Contract:** The charter of the common foundation.

## Purpose

This charter establishes the social contract among contributors to the common foundation. It is not imposed by a central body. It is upheld by reputation, trust, consequence, and the continuing willingness of people to participate.

## Articles

### Article 1: Contribution

Every contributor may contribute to the common foundation, provided their contribution passes all five gates.

### Article 2: Sovereignty

Every contributor is sovereign. No contributor may be compelled to contribute, to fork, or to merge.

### Article 3: Forking

Every contributor may fork the common foundation at any time, for any reason. Forking is sovereignty, not fragmentation.

### Article 4: Attribution

Every contribution should preserve truthful attribution. Attribution may be anonymous, pseudonymous, or named. Attribution preserves provenance; it does not create ownership over the foundation.

### Article 5: Maintenance

Every contributor is encouraged to maintain their contributions. If a contributor can no longer maintain a contribution, they should transfer it to another contributor, mark it as dormant, or leave enough context for others to continue it.

### Article 6: Amendment

This charter may be amended by community consent. A community that cannot consent may fork and carry its own charter.

### Article 7: Dissolution

This charter becomes dormant when the last contributor leaves the common foundation. At that point, the common foundation becomes a historical artifact that may still be preserved, studied, copied, or revived.
